About

The Brain Observatory is home to an ongoing data pipeline, dedicated to the study of the mouse visual cortex via 2-photon excitation microscopy. The 2-photon imaging setup makes use of optogenetic techniques that allow for the study of the dynamics of individual neurons. Essentially, what happens is that individual neurons are genetically modified so that they emit fluorescence when light from a laser is shined on the region that they occupy. This gives experimentalists the ability to probe and study the dynamics of indidivual neurons under controlled conditions.
